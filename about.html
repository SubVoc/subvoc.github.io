Why Sub Vocabulary works

Learning a new language can be a challenging task, but there are many effective ways to make the process easier and more enjoyable. One such way is to use your hearing and visual memory to learn the language through TV series, movies, and other media.

When you watch TV shows and movies in the language you want to learn, you are exposed to the language in a natural and authentic way. You can pick up new vocabulary, grammar structures, and pronunciation by listening to the dialogue and watching the characters interact. This helps you to develop your hearing and visual memory, which are essential skills for language learning.

To get started, choose a TV series or movie that you enjoy and try watching it with subtitles in the language you want to learn. This will help you to make connections between the spoken language and its written form. As you become more familiar with the language, you can gradually remove the subtitles and challenge yourself to understand more of the dialogue.

Overall, using TV series, movies, and other media can be an effective and enjoyable way to learn a new language. With consistent practice and dedication, you can improve your language skills and gain a deeper understanding of the culture and people who speak the language.
